0.The objective & the honest refocus

The PRD's scope rule is blunt: "if it doesn't support a live demo moment, it's not in scope." The demo is one spine, and every link must survive:

lobby recommendation  →  pit-boss review / override  →  Standard-vs-FairPlay 8-hour simulation  →  eval evidence

What the demo must prove (PRD §1): an AI-assisted system can tell unhealthy table states apart from normal poker behavior by combining weak signals — and it recommends and explains; it never accuses or auto-enforces. Three pillars: Table-Health model, Integrity-Pattern lab, and the AI Investigator ("the LLM builds the case; the human judges it").

The refocus, stated plainly

Two things are true at once:

  • The left two-thirds of the spine is genuinely built and computed. Lobby and pit-boss render from real deterministic scores; the three mandatory cases (A/C/E) work end-to-end.
  • The right end of the spine — the AI Investigator (P4) — does not exist yet. There is zero LLM code in the repo, no evidence packet, no eval harness. That is the project's headline thesis ("AI builds the case"), and it is the single biggest gap to a complete demo.

Where we drifted: the recent deep work — the playsim room simulator and the FICO model-builder — is real and good, but neither is on the demo's critical path. playsim isn't even consumed by the demo today (the simulator reads hand-authored fixtures; playsim is a research track wired for a future one-line swap). The model-builder is an offline scoring-research tool. They are side quests relative to the demo. The critical path is evidence packet → AI Investigator → eval.

2.What's still needed to get to a demo

Ranked by how much each blocks the complete story (not by effort):

#GapWhy it mattersShape of the work
1 AI Investigator (P4) — entirely missing It's the project's third pillar and the literal thesis ("AI builds the case, human judges"). Without it the demo's right end is a structured-score UI wearing a guardrail costume — it cannot show the AI explaining its reasoning. New: a Claude call that takes an evidence packet and emits a safe summary (uncertainty language, counter-evidence surfaced, recommends a human action, never accuses). Needs the guardrail prompt + the hard-rule checks.
2 Evidence packet (Contract 3) — missing (blocks #1) It's the seam P4 consumes: case_id, case_type, scores, top_evidence, counter_evidence, uncertainties, recommended_action, allowed_actions. No packet → nothing to hand the LLM. Mostly assembly, not analytics: P3 already produces the scores, reason codes, and counter-evidence (integrity.py). Define the schema (D3) and serialize per case.
3 Eval harness — missing (the "evals" half of P4) The PRD wants the AI's summaries graded: grounding, no-overclaiming, counter-evidence present, always a human action. The EvalPanel UI exists but is fed by structured scores, not LLM output. A small rubric + runner over the 7 seeded cases; render results in the existing EvalPanel.
4 Simulator paths are hand-authored, not computed Demo-safe today (the story holds), but the 8-hour divergence is presenter-authored, not "computed from the simulator." A reviewer may ask "is this real?" Promote playsim output into data/room_metrics_*.json — the schema already matches (a test pins it). This is the one place the playsim work pays off for the demo.

The critical path is short and specific
Evidence packet (#2) → AI Investigator (#1) → eval harness (#3). All three are P4-shaped and currently unstarted. Everything else on the spine already renders. If the goal is "a demo that proves the thesis," this is the work — not more simulator depth.

3.How the demo works & the questions it answers

The walk-through (what the presenter clicks)

  1. Lobby. A player sees neutral "recommended for you" tables — no scores, no labels. Shows: the product is safe to put in front of players.
  2. Pit-boss console. The operator sees the same room with the curtain pulled back: health scores, seating risk, integrity cases with counter-evidence, and a recommended action they can override. Shows: structured scoring finds risk; the human decides.
  3. Standard-vs-FairPlay simulation. Run the room 8 hours two ways — naive seating vs FairPlay routing — and watch the health/retention curves diverge. Shows: the recommendations would change outcomes.
  4. Eval evidence. Across the seeded cases, show the system flags true risks and doesn't over-escalate the look-alikes — and (once P4 exists) that the AI's written summaries are grounded, hedged, and always defer to a human. Shows: it's trustworthy, not a black box.

The one-sentence question the whole demo answers: "Can a system combine weak signals to flag genuinely unhealthy tables — and explain itself safely enough that a human operator, not the machine, stays in charge?" Today we can show the flagging convincingly; we cannot yet show the explaining (the AI half).

4.Recommendation

Build next (in order)

  1. Define + emit the evidence packet for the mandatory cases (A/C/E first). Assembly over P3's existing outputs. P3→P4 seam
  2. Stand up the AI Investigator: one Claude call, evidence-packet-in / safe-summary-out, with the guardrail prompt and the hard-rule checks. P4
  3. Add the eval rubric + runner over the 7 cases; surface in the existing EvalPanel. P4
  4. Promote playsim into the simulator paths (the one place the simulator work reaches the demo) — only after 1–3, and only if time allows. P2/this checkout

Stop polishing (for now)

  • playsim depth — the room loop, behavior models, and routing comparison are already more than the demo needs. The remaining demo value is the single fixture-promotion step (#4), not more behavioral fidelity.
  • The FICO model-builder — a genuinely nice scoring-research tool, but off the demo path. The champion rules already ship the demo.
What not to claim yet
Until P4 exists, do not present the demo as showing "the AI Investigator." The current pit-boss "AI summary" is a structured scorecard with safe framing — true and useful, but it is not an LLM explaining its reasoning, and a sharp reviewer will catch the difference. Frame today's build as "structured scoring with guardrail-safe presentation; the AI-investigator layer is the next milestone."
