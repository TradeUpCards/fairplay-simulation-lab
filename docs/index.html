§01

Overview & Thesis

~5 min
Core thesis: Table health is the liquidity of weak money. Integrity is retention infrastructure. A recreational player who leaves after 12 minutes because the table is predatory is lost revenue — and the loss is preventable if you know before they sit down.

FairPlay IQ solves a detection-and-routing problem, not a punishment problem. The chain is: detect integrity signals → score tables and players → route new seatings toward healthy compositions → explain elevated cases to a human "pit boss" → human decides. The system never auto-accuses or auto-bans.

The product operates at the intersection of two historically separate problems. Mature integrity systems catch specific violations after the fact (bots, multi-accounting, RTA). FairPlay IQ's angle is different: model table health before damage happens, predict how seating a given player changes that health, and surface the cases where something is convergently suspicious.

Measure

Table Health Score

Continuous 0–100 score per table. Measures expected survival of weak money — the recreational cohort's session length and retention, not the table average. Averages hide predation.

Route

Seating Router

Ranked badge per candidate table. Deterministic, auditable policy: Rank = w·Fit + w·Health + w·ΔHealth after an integrity hard-gate. Player can still choose any table.

Explain

AI Investigator

LLM receives a structured evidence packet only — never raw player data — and writes a plain-language case summary with counter-evidence surfaced and a concrete human action recommended.

What this is NOT: Not real-time enforcement. Not auto-ban. Not an RTA detector. Not KYC. Integrity patterns are modeled as scored signals on synthetic data — demonstrating what a signal-aware system would do, not performing real device forensics or OSINT.

§03

Measuring Table Health

~10 min

Table health is not the average player skill or average pot size. Averages hide predation — colluders' winnings and victims' losses cancel. Instead, health measures the recreational cohort specifically: how long are weak players surviving at this table?

Health(T) = 100 − Ppred − Pfrag − Pclus − Pbleed
Each term is bounded [0, 100]; the sum is clamped to [0, 100]. Terms are additive penalties subtracted from a perfect-health baseline of 100.
Composition-driven (Ppred, Pfrag, Pclus): recomputed whenever the seated player set changes.  ·  Observed (Pbleed): derived from live session outcomes; held fixed between seating events.
Ppred Predation Pressure Composition-driven · recomputes on seating
Penalizes tables where aggressive/high-skill players outnumber or outweigh recreationals. The skill-weighted ratio of aggressive_predatory archetypes to new + recreational archetypes, scaled by a configurable pressure curve. One aggressive player at a table of four recreationals is tolerable. Two at a short-handed table is exponentially worse.
range 0–45 · ML regressor or interpretable formula · calibrated from sim outcomes
Pfrag Fragility Composition-driven · recomputes on seating
Penalizes tables with low seat-count and declining paid-seat-time trend. Short-handed tables concentrate exposure. A new player at a 3/6 seat table with volatile players is in more danger than at 6/6. Fragility rises as occupied_seats / max_seats falls and as paid_seat_time_trend = declining.
range 0–25 · deterministic formula · inputs: occupancy ratio, trend label, avg session length in last 6 intervals
Pclus Active-Cluster Severity Composition-driven · recomputes on seating
Penalizes tables where a flagged coordinated cluster is actively seated. Severity scales with the cluster's integrity score and the fraction of open seats occupied by cluster members. A full cluster at a table drives up both integrity risk and predation pressure simultaneously.
range 0–30 · graph aggregation → integrity score → mapped to penalty
Pbleed Observed Bleed Observed · held fixed between seating events
Tracks actual recreational session truncations. When recreational players leave significantly earlier than their archetype-baseline session length, it's a trailing indicator that health is already damaged. Computed from the previous N paid-seat-time intervals. Does not respond to seating changes until sessions play out — which is why composition terms lead and bleed follows.
range 0–20 · rolling window over 6-minute paid-seat-time intervals · requires observed session data

§04

ΔHealth on Seating

~4 min

When considering whether to seat player P at table T, FairPlay IQ computes how Health(T) would change if P were added to the composition. This marginal impact — ΔHealth — is the third term in the router's ranking formula.

ΔHealth(P→T) = Health'(T ∪ {P}) − Health(T)
Only the composition-driven terms are re-scored: Ppred, Pfrag, Pclus.
Pbleed is held fixed — it doesn't change until sessions play out. No per-decision simulation. One re-score of three terms.
Negative ΔHealth

Seating degrades the table

Adding this player increases predation pressure or cluster severity. The router penalizes this table. If ΔHealth is negative enough, the table may drop a badge even if absolute Health is acceptable.

Example: P-104 (new, 14 lifetime hands) → T-22 (two aggressive_predatory players, 3/6 seated). ΔHealth ≈ −8. Adds a vulnerable target without strengthening composition.

Positive ΔHealth

Seating improves the table

Adding a recreational player to a table that lacks recreational depth slightly improves composition. The router rewards this — encouraging placement of players where they also help the table.

Example: P-104 → T-8 (balanced, 5/6 recreational-weighted). ΔHealth ≈ +2. New player adds recreational depth without stressing predation terms.

No per-decision simulation: ΔHealth is computed analytically by re-running the composition-term formulas with the new player included. This is O(1) per table, not a Monte Carlo. The simulation lab exists for offline calibration and eval — not the live routing loop.
§05

The Router Decision

~8 min

The seating recommendation is a deterministic, explainable weighted policy. ML lives upstream (it computes Fit, Health, integrity scores). The routing decision itself is a frozen, auditable formula — no model in the loop.

Rank(P,T) = wfit·Fit(P,T) + wh·Health(T) + wΔ·ΔHealth(P→T)
Subject to: integrity hard-gate — if T has an active flagged cluster pending pit-boss review, the table is removed from the candidate set entirely (not ranked lower — removed). Player can still manually select any table; the badge reflects the recommendation only.
Weights (v0 defaults): wfit = 0.30  ·  wh = 0.40  ·  wΔ = 0.30  ·  Calibrated offline via eval lab.
Fit(P, T)

Player-Table Fit

A 0–100 score for how well player P's archetype, stake preference, and predicted session length match table T's style, stakes, and composition. Produced by an ML predictor. High Fit = player is likely to have a long, enjoyable session.

Output badges

Lobby Recommendation

Recommended for you Rank in top tier
Good fit Rank in mid tier
Available Ranked lower, not promoted
Hidden / gated Integrity hard-gate active

Worked Example — P-104 Seeking a Table

Player P-104 · new · 2 days registered · 14 lifetime hands · vpip 0.62 (loose/passive)
Table Fit Health ΔHealth Rank / Badge
T-8 · $1/$2 NLH 74 81 +2 55.2  Recommended for you
T-14 · $1/$2 NLH 58 67 0 44.2  Good fit
T-22 · $1/$2 NLH 22 38 −8 13.0  Available (not promoted)

T-8: 0.30×74 + 0.40×81 + 0.30×2 = 22.2 + 32.4 + 0.6 = 55.2  ·  T-14: 17.4 + 26.8 + 0 = 44.2  ·  T-22: 6.6 + 15.2 − 2.4 = 13.0

Why the policy is not ML: A routing model trained end-to-end would be a black box — impossible to audit, impossible to explain to a regulator, and impossible to override safely. The weights are calibrated using eval-lab outcomes but the formula is human-designed and frozen. Any operator can inspect it and trace any recommendation to specific factor values.
§06

Where ML vs. Determinism

~8 min

The most important architectural line in the project: ML lives in perception, not decision. The routing policy and the simulator are deterministic and auditable. This is a hard constraint, not a preference.

§07

The Relationship Graph

~8 min

Integrity detection is fundamentally a graph problem. Players, devices, tables, sessions, and seating events are nodes. Device links, timing correlations, co-seating history, and soft-play signals are edges — some raw, some derived. Integrity = convergence of N independent signal edges on one connected component. No single signal is proof.

Substrate: a labeled property graph (Neo4j or Memgraph) — not RDF/OWL. A property graph lets you attach arbitrary key-value properties to both nodes and edges and traverse relationships with Cypher. SPARQL/ontologies are over-engineered for this use case.

Node types (~7)

  • Account — player with archetype, behavioral stats, integrity scores
  • Device — mocked hardware/browser fingerprint group
  • Table — game table with Health score, stakes, composition
  • Session — one continuous table session with paid-seat-time intervals
  • Seating — event node: player requests/is placed at a table. Captures the FairPlay routing decision and outcome.
  • Cluster — derived community node (WCC/Louvain output); holds aggregate integrity score
  • Evidence — structured case packet; linked from a Cluster; fed to the LLM investigator

Signal edge types

  • USES_DEVICE — raw: Account → Device
  • HAS_SESSION — raw: Account → Session
  • DEVICE_LINK — derived: Account ↔ Account sharing a Device
  • TIMING_CORR — derived: Account ↔ Account with correlated session timing
  • CO_SEATED_WITH — derived: Account ↔ Account co-seated at N tables (rate as property)
  • SOFT_PLAYS_AGAINST — derived: Account → Account with EV concession below −0.60 threshold
  • TARGETS_OUTSIDER — derived: Cluster → Account with detected outsider pressure

§09

Production & Geo

~4 min

FairPlay IQ launches Texas-first. Geo is deliberately deferrable — US gaming gates on physical location (where the device is), not residency. The only geo seam in v1 is a nullable field.

Geo Seam (v1)

One nullable field

Every Account, Session, and Seating event carries jurisdiction: "TX" | null. All filtering, routing, and scoring logic gates on this field. Expanding to a new state = populate the field + configure state-specific thresholds. No architectural change.

Stack for Production

No live API for demo

Frozen JSON is the canonical demo source. Production readiness (live graph queries, real-time seat events, streaming session updates) is post-capstone. The capstone demonstrates detection and routing logic on seeded data.

Production architecture (post-capstone target):

  • Graph DB: Neo4j (managed) or Memgraph (open-source). Cypher + WCC/Louvain. Derived signal edges computed by a scheduled graph algorithm job.
  • Event stream: Seat events pushed via websocket from the poker platform. Triggers composition-term recompute for affected tables.
  • Scoring service: TS microservice. Reads graph state, computes Health(T) and ΔHealth, returns ranked badge list. Target latency: <200ms.
  • LLM Investigator: Claude API (Sonnet 4.6 or later). Called on-demand when pit boss opens a case — NOT on every seating event. Input is the structured evidence packet only.
  • Pit-boss console: React web app. Real-time case queue, Health dashboard, simulator comparison panel, eval evidence view.
  • No auto-enforcement: The system writes recommendations and evidence; it never writes to account status, bans, or wallet states.
§10

Evaluation & Simulation Lab

~5 min

The simulation lab is an offline eval lab, not a runtime loop. It generates the population, runs the deterministic 8-hour counterfactual, and produces frozen JSON that the rest of the system runs against.

Ground-Truth Labels

Seeded eval cases

7 eval cases (A–G) with known expected outcomes. True-risk cases (A, C, F, G) must rank above false-positive traps (B, D, E). Eval fails if a trap scores higher than its paired true-risk case.

Counterfactual

Standard vs. FairPlay

Both 8-hour paths start from identical room state (hour 0). Diverge at fixed decision-list checkpoints (5 decisions, hours 0–3). The difference in cumulative paid seat-time and new-player retention IS the demo story.

AI Eval Rubric

LLM-as-judge

Each AI summary is evaluated on: evidence grounding · no overclaiming · health-vs-integrity separation · innocent explanations present · clear human action recommended. Pass/fail per case, visible in the eval panel.
